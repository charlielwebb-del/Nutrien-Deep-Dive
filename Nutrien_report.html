Key End Market Drivers & Macro Context

🌽 Agriculture Fundamentals

US corn acres: 94–96M expected 2026 (vs 91–93M in 2025). Each 1M acres ≈ 500kt NA potash demand.
Brazil: Record soybean production cycle; safrinha corn growing. Just-in-time purchasing behavior from growers limits channel inventory.
Global grain S/D: Stocks-to-use ratios near historical averages post-2025 bumper crop → soil replenishment demand supportive for 2026.
▲ Net positive for crop input volumes

⛏ Potash Market Dynamics

Global shipments: 70Mt (2023) → 72.5Mt (2024) → 74.5Mt (2025) → 74–77Mt (2026E). 4th consecutive year of growth.
NTR capacity: ~20M+ tonne nameplate; ~70% utilization. World's largest producer.
Price benchmarks: Brazil CFR MOP $355–360/t (Q4'25 arrivals, up from $280/t low). SE Asia spot ~$390/t.
Canpotex: NTR + Mosaic control ~35–40% of seaborne trade.
▲ 4th year of demand growth, limited new supply

🏭 Nitrogen Market Dynamics

Chinese exports: Ongoing restrictions limiting global urea supply — supports prices.
Russia sanctions: Limiting European/Black Sea ammonia supply availability.
NTR cost advantage: Henry Hub ~$3.5/MMBtu vs European TTF significantly higher. North American N producers structurally more competitive.
2% global N demand growth expected to continue through 2026.
▲ Supply constraints + NA cost edge = supportive

What Is Actually Driving the Rating?

What Justifies the BUY

1. FCF yield (6%+ at trough) — hardest metric. $2.1B FCF in 2025 against a $33.7B market cap = 6.2% yield. This is trough FCF. At any normalisation of potash pricing, FCF rises materially. A 6%+ FCF yield from a world-class asset with a growing dividend is a genuine value signal independent of cycle.
2. DCF at modest $270/t potash → $87/share (+24%). The key question is whether $252/t (2025A) is the right through-the-cycle price. Historically NTR realised $180–220/t pre-2021, spiked to $370/t+ in 2022, and is now at $252/t. If $250–270/t is a sustainable mid-cycle level (supported by 4 consecutive years of demand growth), DCF says $87. That's the genuine intrinsic upside.
3. Multiple re-rate at 8.5x EV/EBITDA → $84/share. 8.5x is NOT a bullish multiple — it's below Yara, below ICL, and well below NTR's own historical average. If earnings visibility improves as potash recovers, even a modest multiple expansion from 7.3x → 8.5x adds ~$14/share.

How El Niño Impacts NTR's Business

NEGATIVE — During El Niño year: Australia retail (drought → lower crop input demand); shortened planting windows in NA/EU; lower crop protection applications. NTR Australia is part of the ~$1.7B international retail business.
POSITIVE — Brazil (El Niño year): El Niño typically brings wetter conditions to southern Brazil/Cerrado → record soy harvests → NTR's Brazilian retail benefits.
POSITIVE — Year AFTER El Niño: La Niña typically follows → excellent Australian rains → strong crop year → demand catch-up, soil replenishment restocking, higher global grain prices → farmers plant aggressively = UPLIFT for NTR retail and upstream volumes.
